## Overview

As the Digital Marketing & Web Coordinator for Foodring Namwon Seafood Ltd, I led the visual redesign and structural planning of our e-commerce product pages. The primary objective was to transform the online shopping experience by delivering a clean, appetizing, and user-friendly interface that effectively highlights the premium quality of traditional ingredients. By establishing a systematic workflow, I bridged the gap between marketing strategies and digital design.

## Key Contributions

- **E-Commerce UX/UI Design**: Created intuitive layout structures and visually appealing promotional banners tailored to improve customer engagement and conversion rates.
- **Design System Management**: Built and managed a comprehensive, scalable UI workspace in Figma. I effectively organized components, variants, and frames to handle dozens of product lines.
- **Workflow Optimization**: Streamlined the mass-production design process by standardizing design tokens (colors, typography, spacing), significantly reducing the time required to launch new product pages.
- **Digital Content Strategy**: Executed marketing initiatives by combining traditional food aesthetics with modern digital trends to attract a broader customer base.

## Technologies Used

- **Figma**: For building scalable design systems, managing UI components, and prototyping.
- **Adobe Creative Suite**: For high-quality image retouching and graphic asset creation.
- **E-Commerce Platforms**: For deploying and managing final digital content on corporate web pages.

## Challenges and Learnings

One of the main challenges was managing a large volume of diverse product variations without losing visual consistency across the brand. The solution involved shifting from a traditional 'one-off' design approach to a systematic, component-based workflow in Figma. This experience drastically improved my ability to manage complex design architectures and taught me how a well-structured design system can accelerate the entire marketing pipeline.

## Outcome

The newly structured product pages and design system are now actively used across the brand's digital storefronts. This initiative led to faster content deployment cycles, eliminated design inconsistencies, and provided a cohesive, high-quality shopping experience for customers.
